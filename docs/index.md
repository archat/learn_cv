# Complete Computer Vision Guide

Welcome to the **Complete Computer Vision Guide**! This comprehensive resource is designed to take you from a curious beginner to an advanced practitioner in the fascinating field of Computer Vision (CV).

## What to Expect

Computer Vision is the science of enabling computers to "see" and interpret the visual world. It is the driving force behind self-driving cars, facial recognition systems, medical image analysis, and augmented reality.

This guide is structured logically to build your knowledge step-by-step:

1.  **Fundamentals**: Ground-up introduction to images as digital data.
2.  **Classical Image Processing**: Algorithms developed before the deep learning era, focusing on mathematical and structural operations like filtering and convolutions.
3.  **Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)**: The deep learning architecture that revolutionized CV. We will cover classification, object detection, and segmentation.
4.  **Transformers in Vision**: The new standard in deep learning, originally designed for NLP but now dominating vision tasks.
5.  **Advanced Concepts**: Cutting-edge self-supervised and multimodal models like DINO and CLIP.

## Prerequisites

To get the most out of this guide, it is recommended to have:
-   Basic knowledge of Python programming.
-   Familiarity with foundational math (linear algebra, calculus, and probability) is helpful but not strictly required to understand the core concepts.
-   Curiosity and a willingness to experiment!
